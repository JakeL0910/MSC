# Make Spanish Casual — Mission & Website Strategy Report
*Prepared June 2026*

---

## Part 1: Mission Restructuring

### The Problem

The current mission — *"spreading Spanish worldwide, free and accessible resources for learning Spanish slang"* — is too broad to be actionable. It doesn't answer:

- **Who specifically** do you serve?
- **What problem** does MSC solve that Duolingo, YouTube, or a tutor doesn't?
- **What does success look like** in a year, five years?

"Spreading Spanish worldwide" is a statement about scale, not purpose. It doesn't differentiate MSC or give donors, partners, or members a reason to rally behind the org specifically.

---

### Proposed Mission Statement

> **Make Spanish Casual empowers learners — especially young people and heritage speakers — to connect authentically with Spanish by making cultural, conversational, and community-driven language education free and accessible.**

This is tighter because it names:
- **Who:** learners, especially youth and heritage speakers
- **What:** cultural, conversational, community-driven education
- **How:** free and accessible
- **Why it matters:** authentic connection, not rote memorization

Feel free to adapt — but the more specific the who, the more resonant the mission.

---

### Three Mission Pillars (to anchor everything — website, programs, fundraising)

**1. Authentic Language**
Spanish as it's actually spoken: slang, regional expressions, memes, voice notes. Not the textbook version nobody uses in real conversation.

**2. Cultural Belonging**
Language is identity. MSC celebrates the full cultural richness behind Spanish — not just grammar, but community, music, humor, and expression.

**3. Free Access**
Quality Spanish education shouldn't cost money. MSC removes barriers so anyone — regardless of income or background — can participate fully.

---

### Vision Statement (long-term aspiration)

> A world where every learner has access to authentic, culturally grounded Spanish — free of cost, full of community.

---

### What to Do With This

- Replace the current tagline *"Education for Everyone"* (generic) with something sharper that reflects the authentic/cultural angle
- Use the three pillars to organize the homepage, about page, and donation asks
- Anchor fundraising copy to "free access" — donors need to know *why* their money matters

## Part 2: Website Audit — Specific Issues

### 🔴 Critical (Fix Immediately)

**1. Broken counters on homepage**
The stats section shows "0 k People Impacted" and "0 Years of Experience." This is worse than having no stats — it looks like a broken, abandoned site. Either populate these with real numbers or remove the section entirely.

**2. Demo/placeholder blog posts**
The About page sidebar shows:
- *"What are the easy dance form to learn for beginners?"*
- *"Most essential UX design principle for new comers."*

These are leftover from the WordPress theme demo. They are public, indexed content that destroys credibility. Delete them immediately.

**3. Leftover test text on About page**
At the bottom of the About page: *"qsandbox support: test. This is another text :)"* — visible to every visitor. Remove this.

**4. Third-party image links**
Two icons on the homepage (icons 2 and 3 in the "Why learn with us" section) still point to `themegrilldemos.com` — the theme demo site. Upload your own icons so the site doesn't depend on an external domain that could go down.

**5. Outdated copyright**
Footer says "Copyright © 2023." Update to 2026 (or use a dynamic year).

---

### 🟡 High Priority (Address Soon)

**6. Navigation is bloated and exposes backend pages**
The "Learn" dropdown includes: Login, Register, Members, User, Account, Instructor Registration — these are admin/backend pages that shouldn't be in the main nav. A visitor should never see "User" or "Account" as nav items. Clean the nav to:

```
Home | Learn | About | Donate | Partnerships
```

With "Learn" only showing: Free Registration, Courses, Resource Center

**7. Duplicate team bios on About page**
The team is listed twice on the About page — once with the current headshots and once with old placeholder bios from an earlier layout. Delete the second block entirely.

**8. "About MSC" vs "About Us" — redundant structure**
The nav has "About MSC" as a parent with "About Us" as a child. These are confusing and redundant. Consolidate: one "About" page, one nav item.

**9. Homepage headline is generic**
*"Education for Everyone"* could describe any ed-nonprofit on the planet. It does zero work differentiating MSC. Replace with something specific to your angle — slang, culture, community. For example:

> *"Spanish the way it's actually spoken."*
> *"Real Spanish. Free. For everyone."*
> *"Where slang meets community."*

**10. No clear primary CTA**
The homepage has competing calls to action (Learn More, Free Registration, Resource Store, Make a Donation). A first-time visitor doesn't know where to start. Pick **one** primary action — likely "Start Learning Free" — and make it the dominant button above the fold.

---

### 🟢 Improvements (Polish)

**11. Team bios are thin**
Most team members have one sentence. For a nonprofit, your team page is a trust signal. Even a brief 2–3 sentence bio per person (what they bring to the org, why they're passionate about MSC's mission) goes a long way — especially for donors and partners evaluating credibility.

**12. Social proof needs sourcing**
The testimonials from Devin and Braeden are good, but they're also MSC team members (per the About page). Consider adding testimonials from learners who are *not* on the team to avoid the appearance of self-referential quotes.

**13. Blog sidebar on About page is wrong**
The About page has a blog sidebar (Recent Posts, Recent Comments) that belongs on a blog page, not the about page. Remove it from the About template.

**14. Resource categories need descriptions**
The homepage lists 8 resource categories (Slang & Street Spanish, Conversational Confidence, etc.) as icon + title only. Adding a one-line description under each makes the site feel more developed and helps visitors understand what they're getting.

**15. "Partnerships" page should describe the value exchange**
If you want organizations to partner with MSC, the Partnerships page needs to clearly answer: *What does a partnership look like? What does the partner get? What does MSC get?* Right now it's unclear.

## Part 3: Recommended Navigation Structure

### Current (too complex)
```
Home | Learn (8 sub-items) | About MSC (2 sub-items) | Make a Donation | Resource Store (3 sub-items) | Partnerships (2 sub-items)
```

### Recommended
```
Home | Learn | Resources | About | Donate | Partner With Us
```

**Learn** → Courses, Free Registration, Community
**Resources** → Resource Center, All Resources
**About** → About Us, Blog, Contact

This cuts 15 nav items down to ~9, removes backend pages from public view, and gives each section a clear purpose.

## Part 4: Homepage Copy Rewrite (Suggestions)

### Hero Section
**Current:** *"Education for Everyone — Drop the textbooks. Pick up the slang."*

**Suggested:**
> **Spanish the way it's actually spoken.**
> Slang. Culture. Community. All free.
> [Start Learning Free] [See Our Resources]

### "Why Learn With Us" — Suggested copy

**01. Learn Authentically**
Forget the textbook Spanish that sounds robotic in real conversation. We teach slang, expressions, and the cultural context behind what you're actually hearing and saying.

**02. Build Community**
Language is more fun (and stickier) when you learn with others. Collaborate with fellow learners, build slang dictionaries together, and practice in a space that feels like belonging.

**03. Give Back**
Use your Spanish for something bigger. Volunteer your skills for bilingual service projects and help open language access to others — because fluency is better when it's shared.
